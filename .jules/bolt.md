## 2024-05-24 - Numpy Vectorization Over Pandas Concat
**Learning:** Pandas `pd.concat(...).max(axis=1)` is significantly slower (~30x) than calculating row-wise maximums element-wise via NumPy (`np.fmax(...)`) because it incurs DataFrame instantiation and column alignment overhead. Additionally, when translating Pandas `.max(skipna=True)` logic to Numpy, you MUST use `np.fmax` instead of `np.maximum`. `np.maximum` propagates `NaN`s, causing subtle mathematical deviations in downstream indicators that depend on initial rolling calculations (like True Range and ADX/ATR smoothing), while `np.fmax` safely ignores `NaN`s and behaves like Pandas `.max()`.
**Action:** Always prefer `np.fmax`/`np.fmin` over `pd.concat` for calculating row-wise maximums/minimums of small numbers of columns inside hot loops or indicator calculations.
